# Phishing-Attack

<section>
    <h2>Scenario</h2>
    <p>
        Molapo Cybersecurity commissioned a controlled phishing simulation to evaluate the effectiveness of its security awareness program and measure how well users could recognize and respond to phishing attempts. As the assigned penetration tester, I was responsible for planning and executing the assessment in a manner that closely reflected a real-world social engineering attack while remaining within the approved rules of engagement.
    </p>
    <p>
        To support the exercise, I developed a phishing simulation website hosted in a controlled laboratory environment on port 5678. The website was designed to closely resemble a legitimate Molapo Cybersecurity login portal and was used solely for assessment purposes.
    </p>
    <p>
        In addition, a simulated internal “Molapo Cybersecurity Portal” was created to enhance realism. This portal does not exist in the real organization and was deployed exclusively within the testing environment, running locally on 127.0.0.1:1000. It was used to mimic internal system behavior and improve the authenticity of the phishing scenario without interacting with any real organizational infrastructure.
    </p>
</section>

<section>
    <h2>Scope</h2>
    <p>
        The engagement scope was strictly limited to a single designated administrative user. The objective was to assess the target's ability to identify phishing indicators and follow appropriate security practices when interacting with suspicious communications.
    </p>
    <p>
        No other employees, departments, systems, applications, or organizational assets were included in the assessment. Any activities extending beyond the authorized target were considered out of scope and strictly prohibited under the rules of engagement.
    </p>
    <p>
        All testing activities were conducted in accordance with ethical hacking principles, organizational authorization, and applicable legal requirements.
    </p>
</section>
